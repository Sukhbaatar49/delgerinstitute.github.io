Our Mission
"We exist not for any government, party, or interest — but for Mongolia itself."

The Delger Institute exists to cultivate Mongolia's intellectual and democratic foundations through rigorous, independent research and evidence-based policy analysis.

We are committed to strengthening the institutions, norms, and civic knowledge upon which a sovereign, prosperous, and democratic Mongolia depends.

Guided by intellectual integrity and a long-term vision for our nation, the Institute serves one master: the truth.

Nonpartisan · Independent · Mongolia-First

Core Values
I
Intellectual Integrity

We follow evidence, not convenience. Our conclusions are never shaped by who funds us, who governs, or what is politically comfortable.

II
Democratic Commitment

We believe democratic institutions — free elections, an independent judiciary, accountable government — are universal prerequisites for a dignified society.

III
National Service

Every research agenda and every recommendation is evaluated against one question: does this serve the long-term flourishing of the Mongolian people?

IV
Independence

No donor may direct, alter, or suppress a research finding. All funding sources are disclosed publicly. Our independence is non-negotiable.

V
Long-Term Thinking

Mongolia's greatest challenges unfold over decades. We commit to thinking in generations, not election cycles. Prestige is built slowly, through consistency.

VI
Open Access

All Institute publications are freely available to every Mongolian citizen, policymaker, journalist, and student. Knowledge hoarded is knowledge wasted.

Research Focus

The questions Mongolia most needs answered.

🏛️

Governance & Democracy

Institutional reform, electoral integrity, anti-corruption mechanisms, rule of law, and the health of Mongolia's democratic structures.

⚖️

Rule of Law

Judicial independence, constitutional development, legal reform, and the foundations of a society governed by law rather than by power.

🌏

Foreign Policy & Sovereignty

Mongolia's geopolitical position, the third neighbor doctrine, relations with Russia and China, and strategies for preserving national independence.

📊

Economic Policy

Resource governance, economic diversification beyond mining, fiscal policy, and sustainable pathways to broad-based Mongolian prosperity.

Founder & Executive Director

The Delger Institute was founded with a single conviction: that Mongolia's future will be shaped by the quality of its ideas. As a nation at a critical crossroads — navigating resource wealth, democratic consolidation, and a complex geopolitical position — Mongolia needs an institution committed to rigorous, independent, and honest analysis.

The Institute bears the name Delger — meaning to flourish — as both a family legacy and a national aspiration. We exist to help Mongolia flourish.
